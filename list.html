    samosa
    A samosa is a popular and savory pastry enjoyed across many cultures, especially in South Asia. This delectable snack features a crispy, golden-brown exterior, typically made from thin layers of dough that are folded around a spiced filling. The traditional fillings can vary widely, including spiced potatoes, peas, onions, and sometimes meat like lamb or chicken. The flavor profile of a samosa is a delightful blend of spices, such as cumin, coriander, and turmeric, which infuse the filling with a rich, aromatic taste. Often served with chutneys or yogurt on the side, samosas are a favorite at parties, street food stalls, and as a delicious addition to any meal. Their versatility and irresistible taste have made them a beloved treat around the world.
